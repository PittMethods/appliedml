About the Course

This is a synchronous, online course taking place as part of the Pittsburgh Summer Methodology Series on August 8-11, 2022. Each of these four days will run for between 10:30am - 3:30pm (New York Time/EDT/UTC-4:00) with an hour lunch break from 12:30pm - 1:30pm (approximately). Enrollment in the course and access to the video recordings requires registration, which can be purchased through the Pitt Methods website; we are pleased to offer a discounted rate for students and trainees. However, materials (slides and activities) are freely available through this website and may be re-used according to the CC-BY License.

Course Communication

Important announcements will be emailed to registered attendees. However, attendees are strongly encouraged to join the course’s Slack workspace. This workspace will provide a place for questions, answers, comments, and other discussions to occur in a more permanent place. We will monitor the Zoom chat window but this disappears when you leave a meeting, so Slack is the preferred method for communication. There will be several channels for discussion different topics (i.e., announcements, general, questions, resources, and random). More personal or sensitive communications should occur via email or Slack direct messages.

Course Conduct

Etiquette:

  • Behave professionally at all times
  • Stay on topic and minimize distractions
  • Stay muted unless talking (to minimize noise)
  • Ask questions in Slack or use “Raise Hand”
  • Be respectful to everyone in the workshop
  • Be patient with yourself and others

You have the right to:

  • Be treated with respect at all times
  • Turn your camera on or off
  • Arrive and depart whenever needed
  • Ask for help with workshop content
  • Share your opinions respectfully
  • Reuse materials according to the license
  • Receive reasonable accommodations
  • Contact the instructors by email
